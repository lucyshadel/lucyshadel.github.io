Final Project: Soul Eating Bear

Haven’t you ever wanted a little friend who is powered by the souls of the damned? The only problem is getting the souls! This innovative stuffed bear has all the classic traits of a haunted doll, and runs completely on cold, conductive items (a surprisingly good human soul-substitute). Just feed him a marble or a wad of conductive thread!

I created a stuffed bear with an open mouth. I created a homemade switch and a temperature sensor in the bear’s mouth so that the LEDs turn on and get slowly get brighter or darker (in opposite directions) over a period of time when the switch is closed and the temperature is low (i.e. a cold, metal marble). I sewed the materials to the bear using conductive thread, and I decorated with red embroidery thread.

List of Materials: 

2 LilyPad Analog LEDs 

1 LilyPad Computer 

1 LilyPad Temperature Sensor 

Conductive Thread 

Thread/Needle/Scissors 

Red Embroidery Floss 

1 Stuffed Bear 

1 Earplug 

2” by 2” Conductive Fabric 

1" by 1" White Felt

Tip 1: Be mindful of crossed wires! I used a little bit of felt to seperate conductive thread on the back of the bear. 

Tip 2: For coding, creating functions is a great way to split a project into reasonable sections. Use functions! 

Tip 3: Be adaptable. Your first vision will rarely work perfectly, so figure out a back-up.
